The Hard Landing on Mars.
Mars is one of the hardest places for a spacecraft to land. The planet's air is very thin — about a hundred times thinner than Earth's. Because the air is so thin, it cannot slow a falling spacecraft very much. A lander that comes down too fast will simply break apart.
So engineers choose the landing method depending on how heavy the rover is. A light rover can be wrapped in airbags and bounce safely across the ground. But a heavy rover is far too heavy for airbags. Instead, it is lowered gently on cables from a hovering “sky crane.”
